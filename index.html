Half Ironman Challenge

Hi, I’m Niko Scherer, a junior at the University of South Florida. I play basketball and hit the gym sometimes, but I’m not a serious athlete.

A bet with a friend raised a question: could the average person with no training at all wake up one day and complete a Half Ironman triathlon?

On Thursday, May 8th, I’ll attempt a 1.2-mile swim, 56-mile bike ride, and 13.1-mile run, all in a single day. It's all to raise money for UNICEF, a charity providing life-saving aid to children worldwide.

How it works:

  • You place a pledge to donate, but only if I finish the challenge
  • If I succeed, your donation goes straight to UNICEF
  • If I fail, I’ll refund your donation and match it myself. UNICEF still gets the money—you’re just betting on whether I can actually pull this off

The challenge starts at USF’s REC Gym with the swim, followed by the bike and run at Flatwoods Park, a nearby trail in Tampa.
